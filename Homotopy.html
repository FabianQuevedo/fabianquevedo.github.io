Homotopic continuation methods in the solution of systems of equations

Embarking on the exploration of homotopic-continuation methods for root searching, a pivotal domain within computational linear algebra. Delving deep into the intricacies of the homotopy function through rigorous analytical study, we meticulously implement a homotopy method, displacing the study function. Furthermore, we adeptly apply the Newton-Raphson method to homotopy functions, unraveling its profound impact on method convergence. Notably, our research unveils a significant enhancement in convergence rates attributable to the Newton-Raphson method, all while maintaining error levels akin to analytical roots. As an additional facet of our investigation, we meticulously analyze the convergence patterns through Newton fractals (see figures above), shedding light on the method's sensitivity to initial points and auxiliary functions.
